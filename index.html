Hi there. I am a computational materials scientist specialising in quantum and classical simulation for materials discovery.

I combine first-principles (many-body) methods with machine-learning-augmented modelling to design high-throughput simulation workflows. I have defined the scientific strategy and co-led the technical execution of large-scale international research collaborations, working closely with experimental and engineering teams to translate research into applied outcomes — including the Materials Modeling Quantum Complexity Database.

Materials Physics

My core expertise lies in ab-initio methods to predict the electronic (DFT++, DMFT) and structural properties of complex materials systems critical for future energy technologies, including transition-metal oxides, alloys, spin defects, and elemental metals.

Machine Learning & Algorithm Development

I utilise state-of-the-art HPC and workflow management systems to explore these materials. When standard approximations (like DFT) fail, I develop novel, proprietary quantum-enhanced functionals and Monte Carlo solvers. I also design physics-informed machine learning methods as a companion to these simulations that compress and exploit inherent physical properties and symmetries to remove systematic algorithmic errors.

I'm a passionate hill walker, staunch advocate of the slow food movement, and always keen to learn something new.
